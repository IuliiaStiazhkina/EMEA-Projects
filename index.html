IL Invoice Customer Portal

What is the project about?

The Israel operations team aims to provide their VIP customers with a portal to access their official invoices. These invoices will be electronically stamped by Comsign, a distributor authorized by the Israeli government.
The development of this project involves several steps:

  1. 1. Invoice Generation: Invoices are generated in SAP.
  2. 2. Cloud Delivery: SAP sends the invoices to the cloud.
  3. 3. Electronic Stamping: The cloud forwards the invoices to Comsign, where they are being stamped and sent back.
  4. 4. Document Storage: The stamped invoices are sent to the digital document center (DCC), also located in the cloud.
  5. 5. SharePoint Access: Finally, the invoices are sent to the Israeli Operations SharePoint, from where VIP customers can download them.
Our team is responsible for developing the entire backend infrastructure.

What makes it great?

The project has a huge future potential. Upon successful implementation, the knowledge and development procedures can be easily applied to other countries for similar projects.

Egypt Scheduling System

What is the project about?

The Scheduling Tool is a Software as a Service (SaaS) solution developed by P&IT for the EMEA Region. It is designed to support the Cement Operations in Egypt by providing an efficient platform for "Order Prioritization." This tool enables Sales Representatives to effectively manage their quotas, ensuring they do not exceed the cement quantities allocated to specific customer jobsites.
In daily operations, Sales Representatives must regularly review their assigned quotas to prevent any issues arising from selling more cement than is allocated for a particular jobsite. The primary objective for each Sales Representative in Egypt is to meet their daily "target" of cement tons. The Customer Service Team is responsible for assigning quotas to each Sales Representative.

What makes it great?

  • Streamlined Quota Management: The tool simplifies the quota management process by enabling daily or recurring quota assignments.
  • Real-Time Adjustments and Change Logs: If a Sales Representative needs to adjust their quota, the system automatically reflects these changes in real time, maintaining a change log to reduce communication and minimize adjustment time.
  • SAP Integration for Efficiency: Integration with SAP reduces the manual workload previously required by earlier solutions.
  • Enhanced Visibility and Coordination: The tool provides improved visibility across all stakeholders, ensuring better transparency and coordination throughout the process.

Production Reporting Evolution

What is the project about?

This project addresses the significant manual effort involved in generating reports at Cemex plants. Currently, data is collected from various sources, including automated systems (such as sensors) and manual readings (such as inventory checks), requiring substantial time and effort to compile and report.
The focus of this initiative is the evolution of Cemex's long-standing GraphOper tool, which has been in use for over 30 years. This tool is being replaced by the DVM (Data Validation Model) tool to streamline the process. The DVM tool connects directly to systems that automatically capture data from sensors and posts it for validation. Manual entries can also be added as needed.
This solution increases automation, reduces the manual effort involved in data validation, and simplifies the process of committing validated data to SAP, which previously required manual input.
The project initially focuses on Spain and Germany, with plans to expand to the Czech Republic, Poland, Croatia, and the United Kingdom next year.
In parallel, the GES team is working on integrating the DVM tool with SAP, specifically enabling the SAP CC module for cost control. This integration will allow daily cost reporting for operations, a significant improvement over the current monthly or weekly reporting system.

What makes it great?

  • Increased Automation: The DVM tool automates data collection and simplifies its validation, significantly reducing manual effort.
  • Improved Efficiency: Daily data validation and integration with SAP streamline reporting and reduce the time required to enter data manually.
  • Cost Control: Daily cost reports will be available, enabling more accurate and timely cost management.
  • System Integration: The project integrates different parts of the business: production and administrative systems, improving data flow and operational coordination.
  • Enhanced Audit and Reporting: Automation enhances accuracy, transparency, and auditability of data for better reporting and decision-making.

EUR ReadyMix Quality System Phase III

What is the project about?

The EUR ReadyMix Quality System Phase III (RMQ+) project focuses on optimizing and standardizing the cement mix design process across multiple countries in Central Europe, with a key goal of improving both cost-efficiency and environmental sustainability. The initiative was launched in early 2022 to reduce cement content and therefore reducing production cost by 0.25 € / cubic meter, leading to annual savings of approximately €400,000.
The project also addresses the need for better tracking of CO2 emissions for each mix design, aiming to support the company’s sustainability efforts. The RMQ+ system provides a digital platform to standardize ready-mix concrete formulations across plants in Germany, Poland, the Czech Republic, and Croatia, making it possible to optimize raw material usage, reduce costs, and minimize the carbon footprint of production.
Phase I and Phase II focused on building the foundation and initial pilot testing, and in Phase III, the project has introduced advanced features, improved functionality, and increased scalability. Although the rollout was initially planned for 2024, the business decided to enhance the tool further before a broader launch.

What makes it great?
The EUR ReadyMix Quality System Phase III (RMQ+) stands out because it combines operational excellence with environmental responsibility.
Here’s what makes it truly remarkable:

  • Standardization and Consistency: The RMQ+ system ensures that all plants in the region follow a unified and optimized mix design methodology. This standardization enhances the consistency of product quality and simplifies the process for technicians across different locations.
  • Sustainability Focus: One of the project's core innovations is its ability to track and reduce CO2 emissions associated with each mix design. By optimizing the mix formulations and reducing cement content, the project contributes to the company’s goal of lowering its carbon footprint and meeting sustainability targets.
  • Cost Optimization: By using the digital platform to identify the most cost-effective mix designs, RMQ+ helps reduce raw material consumption and production costs. The expected savings are significant, with the potential to save €800,000 annually.
  • Advanced Technology and Automation: The system introduces automation for various stages of the mix design process. Technicians can explore multiple mix alternatives quickly and efficiently, improving the speed and accuracy of decision-making in the formulation process.
  • Scalability and Flexibility: The platform is designed to be scalable across regions, making it adaptable to future growth and expansion. The ability to manage recipes across multiple countries and plants from a central system simplifies the coordination of large-scale operations.
  • Modernization: The RMQ+ system replaces outdated legacy tools, offering a modern, supported solution that can evolve to meet the company’s future needs.
In summary, RMQ+ is not just about optimizing concrete mix designs; it’s a strategic initiative that aligns operational efficiency with sustainability goals, making it a significant contribution to both cost reduction and environmental impact.

PL GB Atradius Credit control Implementation in SAP

What is the project about?

The PL GB Atradius Credit Control Implementation in SAP project is focused on automating and optimizing the process of credit management for Cemex, through collaboration with Atradius, a global credit risk insurance provider. Atradius helps Cemex assess the credit limits of its customers, taking into account their financial standing, payment behavior, and liabilities. Based on this assessment, Cemex can establish the maximum amount of credit they can extend to each customer.
The project involves implementing an interface (API) that connects Cemex’s SAP system with Atradius, enabling real-time updates of customer credit data. This helps Cemex make more informed decisions about extending credit and ensures that customer accounts are regularly monitored for compliance with the established credit limits. The integration is being implemented gradually across different regions, starting with Spain, the UK, and Poland, and expanding to other countries such as Israel and Croatia.

What makes it great?
The PL GB Atradius Credit Control Implementation in SAP project offers several key benefits:

  1. 1. Automation and Real-time Updates: The system automatically updates customer credit limits on a daily basis, eliminating the need for manual intervention, ensuring that credit data is always up-to-date.
  2. 2. Improved Risk Management: : By integrating Atradius’ credit information into the SAP system, Cemex can more effectively manage credit risk. The system helps identify customers who are at risk of exceeding their credit limits, enabling proactive actions.
  3. 3. Global Integration: : The project aligns Cemex’s global credit control processes by using a centralized platform to manage credit information across multiple countries.
  4. 4. Flexibility and Scalability: The implementation is designed to be flexible and adaptable to different country-specific policies regarding credit limits.
  5. 5. Operational Efficiency: The automation of credit management processes reduces the time and resources needed for manual data entry and monitoring.
  6. 6. Financial Security: With Atradius providing a safety net against credit defaults, Cemex can offer more credit to customers without taking on excessive financial risk. This ensures that Cemex can maintain a healthy cash flow while minimizing the impact of potential non-payments.

The combination of operational improvements and user-centric design makes this project not only innovative but a valuable step forward in digitizing cement plant operations.

Process Mining

What is the project about?

Process Mining is a methodology that works as a bridge between Data Science (Big Data) and Process Management. For Aggregates in the UK we are applying this method to read the digital footprint of Order-to-Cash thanks to the traces (logs) left by the execution of processes in SAP SRP and SAP OF.

What makes it great?

What makes this project special is its ability to uncover opportunities for improvement without guesswork. Instead of relying on assumptions or lengthy manual analysis, process mining provides accurately quantified, data-driven insights.
With nearly real-time data processed by technology of Celonis and the collaboration across Process & IT and Business areas, the current effort focuses on reducing manual rework, increasing automation, and identifying patterns that lead to late deliveries.
